by. Zophia A. Rivera

Her loving hands

Before I knew the weight of days, Before I learned of loss or fear, Her hands were there in quiet ways, To pull me close and keep me near. She stitched my name in every prayer, In every hope she chose to keep Her love was present everywhere, A watchful guard while I would sleep. When roads grew long and I grew tall, And thought I did not need her light, She stood behind me through it all, A steady star in darkest night. Her strength was soft, yet never small-- A gentle voice, a constant guide. And even if I drift or fall, Her heart remains right by my side
