The Place You’d Wanna Be
The Sands of Buntod Reef


Where the turquoise water meets the sky,
A ribbon of sand begins to lie,
Buntod Reef emerges from the deep,
While Masbate’s waves their secrets keep,
A golden path where the seabirds fly.

The mangrove shadows, thick and green,
Guard the shore, a silent screen,
Their tangled roots in the salt-cracked mud,
Endure the pull of the rising flood,
The quietest kingdom ever seen.

Beneath the surface, a painted world,
Where coral fans are wide unfurled,
The neon fish in a rhythmic chase,
Dart through the reef with silent grace,
In a liquid dream, softly swirled.
The sun dips low in a copper haze,
Setting the sandbar’s edge ablaze,
The "Islet" whispers to the coming night,
Bathed in a glow of fading light,
A fleeting gift for the traveler’s gaze.

O, sanctuary of salt and wind,
Where the soul and sea are intertwined,
A fragile beauty, wild and free,
The heart of Masbate’s rolling sea,
A piece of peace for all to find.
