Love’s First Breath

by. Mark Ezekiel P. Leonor

Love’s First Breath


Her hair as dark as the night.
Her eyes as bright as the light.
Her lips are too cunning for me to look away.
“I can only dream of her,” one might say.

She eats like a glutton without a care,
When she is hungry, she dreams about eating hares.
She goes to our bakery, ordering ten cakes;
More than enough to appear fake.

But still her voice is as gentle as a lyre.
When I look at her, a love burns inside me like a fire.
There are words I wish to say to her;
One, I wish to satisfy hand-to-mouth.
