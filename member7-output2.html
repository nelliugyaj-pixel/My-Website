by. Zophia A. Rivera

Beatriz Rafaela, The Place You’d Love To See

The clock ticks past midnight’s time, As the sunlight reaches my bedroom. Here I am sleepless as the alarm chimes, Doing my homework as the engine goes vroom. Expecting that I could pass before the deadline, Wondering whether I pass or fail. Every homework feels like writing a headline, With no idea how to keep it on trail. My chest feels tight before each score, As my classmate checks my paper. Yet as the time ticks, fear takes shape, A quiet doubt I can’t escape. But I am more than just a failing student, A failing grade that sometimes makes me blind. Beyond the pressure, past the climb, I’m still a soul not bound by time.
