Do it Scared

by. Mark Ezekiel P. Leonor

Do it Scared


The lights are dim, the air feels thin.
A quivering hand, a shudder within.
Your heart beats fast, your breath is snared.
The word falls silent as the pressure makes you scared.

The doubts will shout; your fears will bite.
They’ll cloud your focus and dim your sight.
Yet courage blooms when none is bared.
It’s in hearts that still feel scared.

For fear is no sign you’re weak or scared;
It’s proof you’ve heard adventurers’ call.
For the brave aren’t the ones who cared;
They faced the storm and did it scared.
