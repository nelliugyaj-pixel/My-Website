The Only Exception

by. Krisnel Faye M. Manlapaz

The Only Exception


I have spent my seasons building glass and stone,
A master of the quiet, a queen of the alone.
To trust is to give someone a blade to hold,
And I have never liked the way the steel feels—sharp and cold.
So I stayed behind the gate, where it was safe and dry,
Watching every honest hand go waving, walking by.

But then there’s you—the glitch in every rule I made,
The light that somehow reaches through the heavy barricade.
You are the "maybe" in a world of "nevermore,"
The only ghost I’ve ever let inside the door.

It’s terrifying, truly—the way I want to lean,
When every instinct tells me to vanish from the scene.
I’m afraid I’ll break the delicate, or stain the bright and new,
That my habit of retreating will eventually cost me you.

But for you, I’ll drop the anchor;
for you, I’ll still the flight,
I’ll trade my old avoidance for the middle of the light.
I am learning how to linger, how to soften, how to stay,
Because loving you is worth the risk of being thrown away.
